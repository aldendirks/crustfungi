Galzinia incrustans

(Höhn. & Litsch.) Parmasto (1965)

Basidiomycota: Agaricomycotina: Agaricomycetes: Corticiales: Corticiaceae: Galzinia

Introduction

Very little information has been published on this genus, which is apparently widespread but rare or rarely collected. In Fungi Europaei corticiaceae – the best general key out there for corticioid fungi – the authors report various shrubs as substrates. This specimen, however, was growing on another fungus, a conk. It is interesting to ponder whether this fungus was using the polypore as a leg up to better disperse spores, or whether the mycelium was actually digesting the polypore for nutrition.

Description

Ecology: This specimen was found on the first day of spring on March 21, 2017 on the undersurface of a conk.

Basidiocarp: The basidiocarp is effused-resupinate and very thin with a smooth to grandinoid hymenophore and thin, fimbriate margin. When fresh, the fruiting body has a ceraceous texture.

Spores: Curved, narrowly cylindrical (cylindrical to allantoid), inamyloid spores; length (4.9) 5.2-6.0 (6.3) x width (1.5) 1.8-2.2 (2.4) µm, Q (2.3) 2.5-3.1 (4.1).

Basidia: Terminal, with 4 sterigmata and a basal clamp, subcylindrical to utriform or constricted, possibly pedunculate; length (14.5) 16.6-24.2 (25.7), largest width (3.9) 4.0-4.6 (4.8), smallest width (2.5) 2.6-3.5 (3.7), ratio of length to largest width (3.6) 3.7-5.8 (6.2); sterigmata length (2.1) 2.2-3.2 (3.4).

Sterile Structures: None.

Hyphae: Monomitic and clamped.

Specimens Analyzed

Galzinia incrustas collected at Grape Island, Boston Harbor Islands National Recreation Area in March, 2017.

The fungus was growing on the pore surface of a polypore.

Note the spreading white margin of Galzinia incrustans and the bumpy texture.

The basidia have a basal clamp and are variable in shape; some seem to be pedunculate, or stalked.

Galzinia incrustans has curved basidiospores with usually one, sometimes none or two or more guttules.
