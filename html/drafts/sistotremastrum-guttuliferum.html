Sistotremastrum guttuliferum Melo, M. Dueñas, Telleria & M.P. Martín

Basidiomycota: Agaricomycotina: Agaricomycetes: Trechisporales: Hydnodontaceae: Sistotremastrum

Introduction

This fungus is microscopic marvel. Unlike most crusts, which have four sterigmata, species in the genus Sistotremastrum typically have six. Seriously, this is a cool thing to see. S. guttuliferum also has hyphae that are full of small oil drops called guttules (hence the species name). Superficially, the creamy white sporocarp is impossible to differentiate from the many other similar looking crusts, especially given its variable appearance.

Described in 2013 from the Macaronesian region (islands in the Atlantic Ocean), the specimens analyzed here are the first reports from North America.

Description

Ecology: Collected late winter, early spring, possibly growing year-round, on the bottom of fallen wood; while first described from Atlantic islands off the coast of Africa, these specimens were found at Webb Memorial State Park in MA, and another specimen with a genetic barcode match of 99% was found in Wisconsin, suggesting widespread occurrence across the Northern Hemisphere.

Sporocarp: Whitish, resupinate, effused basidioma with a smooth to odontioid hymenophore and indeterminate, pruinose (powdery granules, frosty) margin; somewhat ceraceous (waxy) when fresh, sometimes cracked like tiles, becoming cracked and membranaceous when dry.

Spores: Cylindrical to narrowly cylindrical, inamyloid, indextrinoid, acyanophilous, (6.2) 6.5-8.1 (9.3) µm long, (2.5) 2.7-3.1 (3.2) µm wide, with a length:width ratio of (2.1) 2.2-2.8 (3.6).

Basidia: Subclavate to subtubular, sometimes with a slight constriction at the waist, (16.6) 17.6-20.8 (22.0) µm long and (6.0) 6.2-7.0 (7.2) µm wide, 6 sterigmata (4.6) 4.8-5.5 (5.6) µm long, shriveling up as the basidia age.

Sterile Structures: None

Hyphae: Monomitic, clamped at all septa, subicular hyphae somewhat thick-walled, (3.6) 3.9-5.1 (5.3) µm wide, cytoplasm of generative hyphae and/or subicular hyphae filled with small oil drops.
