Subulicystidium brachysporum Boidin & Gilles

Basidiomycota: Agaricomycotina: Agaricomycetes: Trechisporales: Hydnodontaceae: Subulicystidium

Introduction

Subulicystidium is characterized by subulate (tapering to a point) cystidia with rectangular crystals that encircle the cystidia in rows or spirals. And poking out of the top of each cystidium is a hypha, which runs through the center of the barb-like structure. Who knows the adaptive function of these strange microscopic structures, but to me they look like they are used for defense against the many animals that graze on crusts. I don't think they stand much of a chance against a snail's radula, though.

While this specimen corresponds to Subulicystidium brachysporum sensu Boidin and Gilles (1988) according to Ordynets et al. (2018), it is clear that the S. brachysporum morphospecies is highly polyphyletic. This means that things we call S. brachysporum look similar but they are not directly related to each other. It's as if you went your whole life calling someone your sister because you look very similar — like twins even — but when you went to the clinic and got your DNA tested you found that you are much more closely related to the bozo down the road then to your beloved sister. But who says you two can't still be family?

Description

Ecology: Growing on the exposed wood of a fallen Salix (willow) in early summer.

Sporocarp: White, effused, resupinate basidioma with a smooth hymenophore.

Spores: Narrowly cylindrical with a length of (6.4) 7.5-8.8 (9.4) µm, width of (2.2) 2.5-2.9 (3.2) µm, and a length:width ratio of (2.1) 2.7-3.3 (3.5); biguttulate (two oil droplets) situated at the poles of the spore, rarely one or three guttules, (1.2) 1.3-1.6 (1.6) µm diameter, with the two guttules in a spore mostly very similar in size; inamyloid, indextrinoid, acyanophilous.

Basidia: Repetitive basidia with four sterigmata, about 15 µm long x 5 µm wide.

Sterile Structures: Subulate cystidia with spirally arranged rectangular crystals.

Hyphae: Encrusted, subicular hyphae somewhat thick-walled, clamped, with a width of (3.6) 3.9-5.1 (5.3) µm.

Chemical Reactions: IKI- and CB-.
